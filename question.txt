Expected Answer:
"Monopsony" refers to a market structure where there is only one buyer for a
particular good or service. In economics, this term is particularly relevant
in the labor market, where a monopsony employer has significant power over the
wages and working conditions of their employees.

---

Q: Explain monopsony in simple terms.
Expected Answer:
Monopsony is a market structure in which there is a single buyer in a market.
In the context of labor markets, a monopsony occurs when there is only one
employer in a particular industry or geographic area, giving that employer
significant power over the wages and working conditions of workers.
